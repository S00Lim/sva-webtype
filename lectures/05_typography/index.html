Shapes

When typesetting, we have two components to consider. As a typographer, we consider various relationships between them.

  • The black shapes on the page
  • The white shapes on the page

The Overall Website

Consider the pace of dark & light across an entire document or website; how this may signify hierarchy and structure.

  • Black shapes: inversed pages, dense pages
  • White shapes: normal pages, light pages

The (Web)Page

  • Black shapes: blocks of paragraphs, images
  • White shapes: page margins, space between columns, paragraphs, images, all components
  • Relevant general CSS: width, margin, padding
  • Relevant CSS Grid: grid-template-columns, grid-gap, grid-column, grid-row

The Paragraph

  • Black shapes: lines of text
  • White shapes: leading (line-height), heading margins / padding, text indents
  • Relevant CSS: line-height, text-align, text-indent, margin, padding.
  • For alternate text directions: writing-mode: vertical-rl; text-orientation: upright / sideways;

The Line

  • Black shapes: words
  • White shapes: word-spacing, alignment, white-space
  • Relevant CSS: text-align, whitespace, letterspacing.

The Word

  • Black shapes: letters
  • White shapes: letter space, tracking, kerning
  • Relevant CSS: letter-spacing

The Letter

  • Black shapes: black shapes of the letter
  • White shapes: the counterforms and exterior shapes of the letter
  • (Relevant CSS: font-kerning, transform)

Quotation marks

Be careful of the difference between ' and ’, " and ”. For large texts, consider hanging your quotation mark.

“Straight quotes are the two generic vertical quotation marks located near the return key: the straight single quote (') and the straight double quote ("). Curly quotes are the quotation marks used in good typography. There are four curly quote characters: the opening single quote (‘), the closing single quote (’), the opening double quote (“), and the closing double quote (”).”

Matthew Butterick, Practical Typography
